# Goalie Card: Valuation, PE Metrics & Exit Strategy

## Overview
This document outlines the financial framework for scaling Goalie Card from a high-margin coaching business into a premier sports-technology asset. It defines the metrics required to attract Private Equity (PE) and strategic buyers, establishes benchmarks for CIC (the asset holder), and structures the "Portnoy-Style" buyback protection.

---

## 1. Core PE Valuation Metrics
Private Equity firms typically value high-growth technology and "platform" businesses based on a multiple of **EBITDA** or **ARR** (Annual Recurring Revenue), depending on the growth rate and profitability.

### Key Definitions:
- **EBITDA:** *Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.* This is the standard measure of "true operating profit" for a business, removing non-cash items and debt structure. 
- **LTV/CAC Ratio:** *Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost.* PE looks for a ratio > 3:1. It proves that every dollar spent on marketing returns three dollars in profit.
- **Churn Rate:** The percentage of subscribers who cancel each month. For high-valuation SaaS, net revenue retention (NRR) should be > 100% (meaning existing customers upgrade more than they cancel).
- **The "Rule of 40":** For growth-stage tech, your **Growth Rate % + Profit Margin %** should equal 40 or higher.
  - *Example:* 30% YoY Growth + 10% Profit = 40 (Excellent).

To transition from "Small Business" to "PE-Grade Asset," the platform must hit specific financial benchmarks.

## 3. The "Portnoy Rule" (Buyback Protection)
To ensure the founder or CIC retains control of the brand's soul even after a sale, the deal must include specific **Right of First Refusal (ROFR)** and **Buyback Clauses**.

### The Barstool/Portnoy Framework:
When Dave Portnoy sold Barstool to PENN, he negotiated a clause that allowed him to buy the company back for $1 if PENN ever decided to divest or if the partnership no longer aligned with the brand's core values.

#### Implementation for Goalie Card / CIC:
1. **Reversionary Right:** If the acquiring entity (Buyer) intends to shut down the platform, pivot the technology to a different industry (e.g., leaving sports entirely), or file for bankruptcy, CIC has the right to buy back 100% of the IP, brand, and user data for a nominal fee ($1 - $100).
2. **Right of First Refusal (ROFR):** If the Buyer receives an offer to sell Goalie Card to a third party, CIC has 30 days to match that offer and reclaim the asset.
3. **Founder Influence Clause:** A "Key Man" clause that triggers a buyback option if the Buyer terminates the founder without cause, effectively "poison pilling" the deal to ensure the founder remains the face of the brand.

## 4. Benchmarking Internal Progress (The Self-Goal)
To prepare for a sale, we must track "PE-Ready" data inside the platform itself.

- **Developer Goal:** Implement a "Business Health" dashboard in the admin panel (hidden from users) that tracks MRR, Churn, and CAC in real-time.
- **CIC Goal:** Ensure all IP (codebase, AI models, trademarked "Goalie Card" branding) is held by CIC as a separate legal entity from the operating entity.
- **Exit Target:** Reach **$1.5M ARR with 60% gross margins** to trigger a "Series A" buyout or Private Equity roll-up conversation.

---

## 5. Strategic "Opportunity to Buy Back" Scenarios
- **Scenario A (Underperformance):** If the buyer cannot scale the platform to $X within 3 years, CIC has the right to buy back the asset at a 75% discount of the original sale price.
- **Scenario B (Strategic Divergence):** If the buyer is acquired by a larger entity (e.g., ESPN, Fanatics) that does not want the Goalie Card product, the asset is returned to CIC for $1 to avoid "sunsetting" the product.

## 6. Developing the Deals: Benchmark Framework

This framework provides concrete numbers for three primary stakeholders: **Self (Founder)**, **CIC (Asset Holder)**, and the **Acquiring Entity**.

### A. The "Self-Benchmark" (The Personal Exit)
*Goal: Secure personal financial freedom while retaining creative control.*
- **Target:** $150k Personal Take-Home Pay (from $71k Year 1).
- **Metric:** Lower founder "touch" time from 40 hrs/week to 5 hrs/week by automating analysis with the AI Engine.
- **Outcome:** The business becomes a "Passive Cash Cow" that can be held indefinitely or sold as a turnkey asset.

### B. The "CIC-Benchmark" (The Holding Co. Protection)
*Goal: Maximize the 100% ownership equity value.*
- **Target:** 75% Operating Margin (EBITDA).
- **Metric:** Acquire 1,000 digital-only Users at a CAC of < $45.
- **Outcome:** CIC builds a balance sheet of IP and user data that is "platform-agnostic" (meaning it could be moved from Supabase to another provider without losing value).

### C. The "Buyer-Benchmark" (The Exit Deal)
*Goal: Create a $10M+ acquisition profile.*
- **Target Profile:** A buyer like **Win Reality**, **GameChanger**, or a **Private Equity Roll-up** (e.g., merging multiple individual-sport apps).
- **Deal Benchmark:**
  - **Upfront Cash:** $5M - $8M (based on 10x EBITDA).
  - **Earn-out:** Additional $2M based on hitting $5M ARR within 24 months post-acquisition.
  - **Equity:** 5-10% rollover equity in the Parent Company for the founder to participate in the "Next Phase" of growth.
  - **The "Portnoy" Backstop:** Contractual right for CIC to buy back 100% of the Goalie Card brand and IP for $1 if the Parent Company declares bankruptcy or decides to sunset the vertical.
